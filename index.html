Case Studies

  • Business Consulting

    Building a team for a business client

    I was asked to completely overhaul a team of ~10 people for one of my business clients (GroundZero Gaming). They had come to employ my services as I had a pre-existing reputation for being able to identify the required skill-set for the job. They had finished in 7th place the previous year, and had never even come close to a top 5 position in their history. There was also a shortage of readily available talent in some of the roles, as it was late into the off-season.

    I knew that I needed to find people who were skilled enough for the roles required, and also needed to ensure that the team environment would be a productive and healthy one. To do this, I needed to assess the personality of each team member, and how they would work together. I also had a limited budget to play with, and needed to ensure everybody was happy. The budget was about 300% smaller than the largest competitor, so it was a tall task.

    Initial screening mostly invovled screening for skills and aptitude. The interview process then involved a personality assessment. I reached out to previous colleagues/managers of the people of interest, and enquired specifically about growth mindsets, high levels of self-accountability, and self-drive. I found a member who ticked all the boxes and seemed like an exceptional candidate, so I ended up allocating a large chunk of the budget to that person. As a result, I had to settle for less-than-ideal members in other positions. I figured that since the exceptional candidate displayed strong leadership skills, I wanted to empower him to elevate the skillsets of the other members.

    Despite the significant financial disadvantage and a history of poor results, the team managed to take home two back-to-back championships in 2024, the first time in history for the organisation. Off the back of the success, the team managed to secure a spot in international competition, and now compete in Asia. The team also reported an increase of 800% in prize money earnings for 2024, compared to the previous year.

  • Leadership

    Poorly performing team member

    During my time on GroundZero Gaming, I noticed that the performance of one of my team members was lagging behind the rest of the team.

    Having spent months together at this point, I had come to realise that this team member places a lot of importance on his performance and holds himself to a very high standard. I figured that he was the kind of person to overthink his own performance as he strives for excellence, and this would end up having an adverse affect. I pulled him aside for a private conversation, without letting any of the other team members know.

    I opened the conversation by highlighting some of the things I thought he did really well. He thanked me, and let me know that he was under alot of pressure because he felt as though he would get replaced, since he was lagging behind the rest of the team. I reassured him that he was chosen for a reason, and that every single person on the team believes in him. I then told him that it is normal to have flucuations in performance, and that we win and lose as a team, and that I want him to focus on just having fun.

    The following day in practice, it seemed like a huge amount of pressure was relieved off his shoulders. I then made sure to give him postive words of affirmation during practice, and the other team members started to join in as well. He thanked me afterwards and our relationship was strengthened as a result.

  • Leadership

    Problematic team member

    One of my team members was severely underperforming, and was being disruptive to the team environment. I had already privately spoken to him on 3 occasions prior regarding his behaviour and performance. In this particular case, he was starting to get quite defensive and aggressive during a routine team review, and it was affecting the other team members.

    I immediately tried to descalate the situation by speaking in a calm yet firm voice and addressing the team member directly. He then started to insult me. I then reprimaned his behaviour in front of all the other team members. I made sure to focus on his behaviour, and not on who he is as a person. I refrained from using 'you' statements, in order not to offend him. I felt as though that it was time to reprimand him publically in order to set an example, and that any sort of disruptive behaviour and disrespect towards other team members was unacceptable. I told him to take a 10 minute break.

    I then had a private conversation with him and informed him that his behvaiour was unacceptable. I also mentioned that I knew that he is a very good person and that I love working with him as a teammate. I emphasised that healthy disagreements between teammates are not only encouaraged but absolutely necessary in building a positive and high-performing team environment, whereas disruptive behaviour is unacceptable. I assured him that the way that he felt was completely valid and understandable, and that I was proud of him for having the courage to express disagreement, but I needed him to express it in a much healthier way.

    He then apologised to the other team members and resolved to improve the way that he communicates moving forward. I directed him to a resource that detailed how to express negative emotions in a healthy way, especially in the workplace. His communication and performance improved as a result over the coming weeks.
